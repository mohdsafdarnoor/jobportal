Job Search Guidelines

Practical guidance for international students seeking employment in Germany

Part-Time Jobs (Werkstudent)

As an international student in Germany, you can work up to 20 hours per week during the semester and full-time during semester breaks.

Where to Find Part-Time Jobs

  • JobMensa: Student-focused platform with local part-time opportunities
  • StepStone: Upload your CV and let recruiters find you
  • Indeed: Broad job search with filters for students
  • University Career Center: Check your university's job board

Application Process

  1. Prepare a German-style CV (Lebenslauf) - typically 1-2 pages, photo optional but common
  2. Write a cover letter (Anschreiben) in German if the job ad is in German
  3. Include relevant academic projects if you have no work experience
  4. Apply through company websites or job portals
  5. Follow up after 1-2 weeks if you don't hear back

Legal Requirements

  • Work permit: Check your visa conditions (most student visas allow 120 full days or 240 half days per year)
  • Tax ID (Steueridentifikationsnummer): Apply at your local tax office
  • Health insurance: Must be enrolled during employment
  • Registration (Anmeldung): Must be registered at your local address

Internships (Praktikum)

Internships in Germany typically last 3-6 months and can be mandatory (Pflichtpraktikum) or voluntary (freiwilliges Praktikum).

Where to Find Internships

  • Handshake: University-connected internship platform
  • LinkedIn Jobs: Filter by "Internship" and location
  • Company websites: Check career pages of target companies directly
  • meinpraktikum.de: German internship portal with reviews

Application Timeline

  • Large companies: Apply 3-6 months in advance
  • SMEs and startups: Apply 1-3 months in advance
  • Rolling applications: Apply as soon as you find a match

Key Application Documents

  1. CV with relevant coursework and projects
  2. Cover letter explaining motivation and fit
  3. Academic transcript (if requested)
  4. References (if available)

Master Thesis with Company

Many students in Germany complete their Master thesis in collaboration with a company, combining academic research with practical business problems.

Finding Thesis Opportunities

  • Excelerate: Platform connecting students with thesis-offering companies
  • Studyond: Thesis project matching service
  • Thesisforyou: Database of company thesis topics
  • Direct contact: Reach out to companies where you've worked as Werkstudent

How to Pitch Your Thesis

  1. Identify a business problem the company faces
  2. Frame your thesis as solving this problem
  3. Emphasize low supervision effort needed
  4. Clarify university requirements and timeline
  5. Discuss data access and confidentiality early

Timeline

  • Start searching: 6-9 months before thesis start
  • Initial contact and topic discussion: 4-6 months before
  • Formal agreement and university approval: 2-3 months before

Full-Time Jobs After Graduation

After completing your degree, you have 18 months to find a job related to your field of study (Job Seeking Visa).

Where to Search

  • EURES: European-wide job portal
  • XING: Professional network for DACH region
  • LinkedIn: International professional network
  • StepStone: Major German job board

Application Strategy

  1. Optimize CV for ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems)
  2. Tailor each application to the specific role
  3. Network through LinkedIn and XING
  4. Attend career fairs and company events
  5. Consider recruiting agencies specializing in your field

Work Permit Transition

  • Job Seeking Visa: 18 months to find a job related to your degree
  • EU Blue Card: For highly qualified workers (salary threshold applies)
  • Work permit: Standard work authorization after job offer

General Tips for Success

  • Learn German: Even basic German significantly increases job opportunities
  • Network actively: Attend meetups, conferences, and university events
  • Customize applications: Never send generic CVs or cover letters
  • Follow up: Send polite follow-up emails after applications and interviews
  • Leverage AI tools: Use tools from this portal to optimize your applications
  • Be patient: The German hiring process can take 4-8 weeks from application to offer
